## Summary
The NVIDIA Accelerated Quantum Computing Research Center, also called NVAQC, is NVIDIA's research facility for shortening the path to useful quantum computing. It integrates partner quantum hardware with NVIDIA AI supercomputing, including GB200 NVL72-class Grace Blackwell systems and the NVIDIA DGX Quantum architecture, and uses [[CUDA-Q]] to orchestrate hybrid quantum-classical workflows.

## Detail

### Purpose
NVAQC exists to help solve the hard integration problems between today's QPUs and future useful quantum computers: quantum error correction, hardware simulation, realtime control, hybrid algorithm development, and coupling quantum systems to accelerated supercomputers.

### Platform role
- Integrates partner quantum hardware with NVIDIA AI supercomputing.
- Public NVIDIA material describes GB200 NVL72 Grace Blackwell systems as part of the facility direction.
- Uses [[CUDA-Q]] for orchestrating quantum-classical systems.
- Connects directly to [[NVIDIA-DGX-Quantum]] architecture language and current [[NVIDIA-NVQLink]] integration direction.
- Targets QEC, hybrid application development, QPU design simulation, and AI-for-quantum research.

### NVIDIA context
NVAQC is where the quantum software stack becomes a full system problem: [[CUDA-Q]], [[CUDA-QX]], [[NVIDIA-NVQLink]], [[NVIDIA-DGX-Quantum]], [[NVIDIA-GB200-NVL72]], and NVIDIA networking all meet partner quantum hardware.
