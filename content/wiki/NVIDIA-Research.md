## Summary
NVIDIA Research is NVIDIA's central research organization, comprising over 300 scientists and engineers working on fundamental advances in AI, computer graphics, computer vision, robotics, and high-performance computing. NVIDIA Research has produced landmark contributions including NeRF (Neural Radiance Fields), Instant NGP, GauGAN/Canvas, StyleGAN, GET3D, and foundational work on neural rendering, diffusion models, and large-scale training infrastructure. Research outputs regularly feed into NVIDIA products and are published at top venues (CVPR, NeurIPS, ICCV, SIGGRAPH).

## Detail

### Purpose
NVIDIA Research advances the state of the art in areas aligned with NVIDIA's long-term product and platform strategy, providing a pipeline from fundamental research to product integration. It also enhances NVIDIA's reputation as a technical leader, attracts top talent, and establishes early leadership in emerging areas before they become mainstream.

### Key Features / Research Areas
- **Neural Rendering**: NeRF, Instant NGP, 3D Gaussian Splatting contributions, deformable neural fields
- **Generative AI**: StyleGAN, StyleGAN2/3, GauGAN, GET3D, Edify (3D/image diffusion)
- **Autonomous Vehicles & Robotics**: CALM, DriveX, robot learning from demonstrations
- **NLP & LLMs**: Nemotron, SteerLM, NVLM, EAGLE research teams
- **Computer Vision**: Video understanding, object detection, 3D perception
- **HPC & Systems**: NCCL algorithms, memory systems, compiler research
- **Healthcare AI**: Medical imaging, drug discovery AI research
- **Physics simulation**: Warp physics engine, differentiable simulation

### Key Labs
- **NVIDIA Research Labs** (Santa Clara, Seattle, Toronto, Austin, Tel Aviv, Zurich, Beijing)
- **Toronto AI Lab (TAIL)**: generative AI and computer vision
- **Seattle Lab**: robotics and embodied AI
- **Autonomous Vehicles Research**: self-driving AI
- **Applied Deep Learning Research (ADLR)**: LLMs, speech, and NLP

### Notable Publications / Contributions
