## Summary
NVIDIA GTC (GPU Technology Conference) is NVIDIA's flagship annual developer and technology conference, serving as the primary venue for NVIDIA product announcements, technical sessions, research presentations, and industry networking. Founded in 2009, GTC has grown from a GPU computing conference into NVIDIA's primary platform for announcing new GPU architectures, AI platforms, software frameworks, and strategic partnerships. The event features Jensen Huang's keynote as a major industry event, alongside hundreds of technical talks, workshops, and hands-on labs.

## Detail

### Purpose
GTC serves multiple strategic purposes: it is NVIDIA's platform for major product launches (new GPU architectures are typically announced at GTC), a developer education and community event, an industry analyst briefing venue, and a partner ecosystem showcase. For developers, GTC provides access to the latest technical knowledge about the NVIDIA stack directly from NVIDIA engineers and researchers.

### Key Features
- Annual conference: held spring (San Jose) with additional regional events throughout the year
- Jensen Huang keynote: major product and architecture announcements (Hopper, Blackwell, etc. all announced at GTC)
- Session tracks: AI, Data Center, Gaming, Design/Visualization, Networking, Robotics, Healthcare, Automotive
- Hands-on labs: connect-the-dot labs powered by NVIDIA LaunchPad
- Research presentations: NVlabs and NVIDIA Research paper sessions
- Expo floor: partner showcases and NVIDIA product demonstrations
- Free virtual attendance: all recorded sessions available online post-conference
- Instructor-led workshops: deep-dives on CUDA, TensorRT, NeMo, Omniverse, and other platforms
- Session count: 1,000+ sessions per annual GTC

### Notable GTC Announcements
- GTC 2022: Hopper H100 architecture
- GTC 2023: Spectrum-X, DGX SuperPOD H100
- GTC March 2024: Blackwell B200, GB200 NVL72, Spectrum-X, NVIDIA Inference Microservices (NIM)
- GTC March 2025: Blackwell Ultra, NVIDIA Cosmos, updated NIM portfolio

### Use Cases
- Staying current on NVIDIA GPU architecture roadmap
- Learning best practices for CUDA-X libraries, NeMo, and AI platforms
- Networking with NVIDIA engineers and researchers
- Evaluating new NVIDIA products before purchase
- Academic researchers presenting GPU-accelerated work

### Hardware Requirements / Compatibility
